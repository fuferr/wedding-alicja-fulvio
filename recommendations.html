Napoli Tips
✦

Travel · Accommodation · Food · Experiences

We have spent many hours exploring Napoli and want to share the places we love most. Make the most of your trip — this city will steal your heart.

✈️

Getting There

1

Fly into Naples (NAP)

Naples International Airport — Capodichino is the closest airport, about 7 km from the city centre. Direct flights from most European cities.

2

Airport to City Centre

Taxi — Fixed rate ~€25 to the city centre (official yellow or white taxis only).
Alibus — Airport shuttle bus to Piazza Garibaldi & Molo Beverello, €5, ~30 min.
Car hire — Available at the airport but driving in Naples is an adventure. We recommend staying central and walking.

3

Alternative: Train from Rome

High-speed Frecciarossa from Roma Termini to Napoli Centrale — just 1h 10min, from €20. A scenic and stress-free option if flying into Rome.

4

Getting Around Naples

The city centre is very walkable. For longer trips, use the Metro Line 1 (excellent) or taxis. Apps: itTaxi (official taxi app), Google Maps works well for transit directions.

🏨

Where to Stay

We recommend staying in the historic centre (Centro Storico) or the elegant Chiaia neighbourhood — both beautiful, safe and full of life. Book early: September is peak season in Naples.

Luxury

Grand Hotel Vesuvio

Iconic five-star hotel on the seafront with views of Castel dell'Ovo and Vesuvius. An unforgettable Naples experience.

📍 Via Partenope, Chiaia ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Boutique

Costantinopoli 104

A hidden gem in the historic centre — a Liberty-style palazzo with a pool, garden and intimate atmosphere. Our personal favourite.

📍 Via S. Maria di Costantinopoli ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Design

Palazzo Decumani

Stylish hotel at the heart of the historic centre, walking distance to everything. Great value and beautifully designed rooms.

📍 Piazzetta Giustino Fortunato ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Mid-range

Hotel Piazza Bellini

Charming hotel on one of Naples' most beautiful piazzas. Friendly staff, great breakfast and right in the artistic quarter.

📍 Piazza Bellini, Centro Storico ⭐⭐⭐⭐
Seafront

Hotel Excelsior

Classic waterfront hotel in Chiaia. Elegant rooms, rooftop terrace bar and spectacular views across the Bay of Naples.

📍 Via Partenope, Chiaia ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Budget-friendly

Airbnb — Chiaia or Posillipo

For those travelling in groups or staying longer, a self-catered apartment in Chiaia or Posillipo offers great value and a local feel.

📍 Chiaia / Posillipo 🏠 Apartment

Where to Eat

You are in the birthplace of pizza. Eat it everywhere — but here are our other favourite spots across the city.

Pizza — iconic

L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele

The most famous pizza in the world. Only two choices: Margherita or Marinara. Queues are long — arrive early or use their new online reservation system.

📍 Via Cesare Sersale 1 💶 Budget
Pizza — modern

Sorbillo

A beloved Neapolitan institution. Creative takes on the classic pizza in a lively, authentic setting. Multiple locations across the city.

📍 Via dei Tribunali 32 💶 Budget
Fine dining

Il Comandante

Michelin-starred restaurant atop the Romeo Hotel with stunning bay views. A truly special occasion dinner — book well in advance.

📍 Via Cristoforo Colombo 45 💶💶💶 Upscale
Seafood

Ristorante Umberto

Historic restaurant in Chiaia serving the finest Neapolitan seafood. The linguine alle vongole is extraordinary. Reserve ahead.

📍 Via Alabardieri 30–31 💶💶 Mid-range
Aperitivo

Caffè Gambrinus

Naples' most historic café, open since 1860. The perfect spot for a Campari spritz or an espresso taken standing at the bar like a true Napoletano.

📍 Via Chiaia 1–2 💶 Budget
Lunch & views

Ristorante La Stanza del Gusto

A charming two-floor restaurant in the historic centre. Creative Neapolitan cuisine at excellent prices. Perfect for a long, lazy lunch.

📍 Via Santa Maria di Costantinopoli 💶💶 Mid-range

Things to Do

Must-do

Pompeii & Vesuvius

A 30-minute train from Naples (Circumvesuviana line). Walk the ancient streets of Pompeii and hike up Vesuvius for the views of a lifetime.

📍 30 min from Naples 🚂 Train
Day trip

Positano & the Amalfi Coast

Take a ferry or drive along the most dramatic coastline in the world. Pastel villages, crystal sea, and lemon trees everywhere. Unmissable.

📍 1.5–2h from Naples ⛴ Ferry or car
Island escape

Capri or Ischia

Both islands are reachable by ferry from Molo Beverello. Capri is glamorous and dramatic; Ischia is lush, thermal and relaxed. Both are paradise.

📍 Ferry from Naples port ⛴ ~1h
Culture

National Archaeological Museum

One of the world's finest collections of Greco-Roman art, including treasures from Pompeii. Worth at least half a day. Book tickets in advance.

📍 Piazza Museo Nazionale 19 🎟 Book ahead
Underground

Napoli Sotterranea

Explore the ancient Greek and Roman tunnels beneath the city. A fascinating, atmospheric tour — highly recommended for curious travellers.

📍 Piazza San Gaetano 68 🎟 Guided tour
Neighbourhood walk

Spaccanapoli & Quartieri Spagnoli

Get lost in the narrow streets of the historic centre. Street art, tiny shrines, coffee shops, and the real pulse of Neapolitan life. No map needed.

📍 Centro Storico 🚶 On foot

A Few Tips from Us

☕ Coffee culture

Espresso is taken standing at the bar and costs €1–1.20. Ordering a "cappuccino" after noon is socially frowned upon by locals — but you're on holiday, so enjoy!

🛵 Safety

Naples is very safe for tourists in the main areas. Keep bags close in busy streets, be mindful of mopeds, and enjoy the city with confidence.

🌡 September weather

Warm and sunny — expect 25–30°C. Evenings can be breezy near the water. Perfect wedding weather. Pack light layers for the nights.
